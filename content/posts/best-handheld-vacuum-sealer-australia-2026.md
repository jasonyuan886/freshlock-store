## How we judged each sealer

We didn't score these on fancy LED displays or app features. We used the criteria that actually affect whether you'll use the thing three months from now:

1. **Suction power (kPa)** — 50–60 kPa is the sweet spot for handhelds; anything under 40 kPa struggles with moist food like marinated meat.
2. **True portability** — cordless? Pocket/drawer-sized? Or just "compact" but still tethered by a cord and heat bar?
3. **Battery & charging** — USB-C is non-negotiable in 2026. Proprietary chargers get lost.
4. **Ongoing bag cost** — the sealer is a one-time purchase; the bags are forever. Cheap sealer + expensive proprietary bags is a trap.
5. **Heat bar vs valve bags** — heat sealers seal flat bags with heat. Handheld pump sealers (like FreshLock) use zipper bags with a one-way air valve, no heat required. Each system has pros and cons.
6. **Availability & support in Australia** — buying off a random Amazon listing with a US plug and no reply to emails is a fast track to landfill.

## 2. FoodSaver Cordless Handheld (FM1400 / FM2100 series)

FoodSaver is the name everyone knows, and their cordless handheld wand is widely stocked in Australia through Big W, Harvey Norman, Amazon AU and major supermarkets.

**The reality:** The handheld wand is a rechargeable pump designed to pair with FoodSaver-branded zipper bags and vacuum containers. It does not have a heat bar. Suction is on the mild side (~40–45 kPa in independent tests), which is fine for deli meat and cheese but can struggle to pull a tight vacuum around dense or moist food like marinated chops.

**Pros:**
- Easy to find in Australian retail
- Rechargeable
- Compatible with a wide range of FoodSaver containers and marinating canisters

**Cons:**
- Ongoing bag cost is noticeably higher than generic or DTC zipper bags — FoodSaver-branded zip bags are among the priciest per litre on the market
- Suction is weaker than 60 kPa handhelds; meat often ends up loosely packed rather than tight
- Older models used proprietary charging docks that are easy to lose
- Some users report the battery degrading noticeably after 12–18 months

**Who should buy it:** If you already own a countertop FoodSaver and a drawer full of FoodSaver zip containers, adding the handheld wand as a portable accessory makes sense. For a first-time buyer starting from scratch, there are better-value options.

## What to look for before you buy

Still not sure? Ask yourself these five quick questions:

1. **Do I want to use custom bag rolls (sous vide, big cuts of meat)?** Get a heat-bar model like the INKBIRD VS-06.
2. **Will I take this camping/to the BBQ/on road trips?** Prioritise a small, light, cordless pump like FreshLock Pro.
3. **Am I going to reuse bags a lot?** Zipper valve bags (pump sealers) are reusable many times; flat heat-seal bags are effectively single-use for raw meat.
4. **How much will bags cost me over a year?** Factor this in. A cheap sealer with expensive disposable bags ends up pricier than a mid-range sealer with reusable bags.
5. **Can I charge it with the cable I already own?** USB-C is the answer you want in 2026. Micro-USB and proprietary docks are a pain.
